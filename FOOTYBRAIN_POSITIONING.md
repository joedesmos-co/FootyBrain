# FootyBrain positioning (product identity)

> **Status:** Public beta positioning (frontend-only, local data).  
> **Authority:** [PROJECT_BRIEF.md](./PROJECT_BRIEF.md)  
> **Tagline:** **Learn the game. Know the players. Become a real fan.**

## What FootyBrain is

FootyBrain is a **learning-first football database** with quiz elements.

It’s designed for the moment when you’re watching a match and realize:

- you recognize the crest and the kit,
- but you don’t know the players, their roles, or why a rivalry matters.

FootyBrain is the bridge from “I watch big games” to “I can follow the sport week to week.”

## What makes FootyBrain unique

- **Learning-first, not news-first**: the primary output is understanding (names, roles, teams, context), not live updates.
- **Names you can retain**: quizzes + hints are built to turn recognition into recall.
- **Curated pathways**: collections and learning paths guide what to learn next without overwhelming the user.
- **Honest scope**: the app is explicit about being a **snapshot** (not a live transfer feed).
- **Single-player registry**: one player profile reused across teams/nations (avoids duplicates; improves learning consistency).

## Who it’s for (ideal beta audience)

**Primary:** casual to intermediate football fans who want to learn:

- friends who watch big tournaments but don’t follow leagues weekly
- new club supporters trying to learn a squad fast
- people who enjoy “study mode” sports browsing (collections, compare, quizzes)

**Secondary:** committed fans who like:

- a clean database view,
- quick quizzes for recall,
- a curated “what should I learn next” panel.

**Not for (yet):**

- users seeking live scores, xG feeds, injury news, transfer rumors
- users expecting full coverage of every league and squad with daily updates

## Category framing

FootyBrain sits between:

- **Live match apps** (Sofascore/FotMob): real-time utility
- **Reference encyclopedias** (Wikipedia): deep text, non-guided learning
- **Games** (quiz apps): recall practice without context

FootyBrain is **guided learning**: context + curated exploration + practice.

## Differentiation (vs common alternatives)

### Vs Sofascore / FotMob

- They optimize for **what is happening now** (scores, lineups, form, alerts).
- FootyBrain optimizes for **what you can learn and remember** (roles, players, rivalries, club/nation identity).
- FootyBrain intentionally avoids “always fresh” promises in beta.

### Vs Wikipedia

- Wikipedia is comprehensive but not structured for practice.
- FootyBrain turns information into a **learning loop**: browse → compare → quiz → save → progress.

### Vs Transfermarkt-style databases

- FootyBrain does not scrape/hotlink images or depend on brittle third-party URLs.
- FootyBrain uses a single internal registry and local data to keep learning consistent.

## World Cup positioning (honest + exciting)

World Cup is a **learning accelerator**:

- featured nations and group orientation (prep)
- tournament legends and stars as entry points
- national-team quizzes (gated by quiz-ready pools)

**Important:** in public beta, World Cup is a **prep hub** — no fixtures, brackets, or live scoring.

## Tone and style guidelines

- **Voice**: confident, friendly, modern sports database — not meme-y, not overly academic.
- **Promises**: never imply live feeds, complete coverage, or real-time transfers.
- **Trust**: always reinforce “local snapshot” + date; device-only storage; no accounts.
- **UX**: mobile-first, uncluttered, action-oriented (open squad / learn / quiz).

## One-sentence pitch

FootyBrain helps you **learn football faster** by turning squads, roles, and rivalries into **guided study + quizzes**—not another live scores app.
