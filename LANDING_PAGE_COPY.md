## Problem → solution

### The problem

Football has thousands of players. You can watch big matches and still feel lost:

- Who is that midfielder? What role do they play?
- Which clubs matter in this league?
- Why do fans care about this rivalry?

### The solution

FootyBrain turns football knowledge into a loop:

**Browse → Save → Learn → Quiz → Repeat**

You’ll learn names, positions, clubs, and national teams through curated collections and lightweight quizzes.

## What makes FootyBrain different

- **Not a live score app**: no match alerts, fixtures, or transfer tickers.
- **Not a wiki**: less scrolling, more guided learning.
- **Practice built in**: quizzes help you retain what you browse.
- **Honest scope**: the app shows the dataset snapshot date and keeps your progress on-device.

## Core features (cards)

1. **Browse database**  
   Players, clubs, leagues, national teams.

2. **Collections**  
   Curated learning playlists (legends, captains, golden generations, more).

3. **Compare**  
   Side-by-side players or clubs to understand roles and strengths.

4. **Quiz mode**  
   Hints-first recall practice. National-team quizzes are gated by quiz-ready pools.

5. **Today’s Picks**  
   A daily, lightweight route into the database.

6. **World Cup prep hub**  
   Featured nations, groups orientation, tournament legends and stars—prep only (no live scores).

## Data & trust (FAQ-style)

### Is this live?
No. FootyBrain uses a **local snapshot dataset** (date shown in-app). It is built for learning, not real-time updates.

### Do I need an account?
No. Favorites, progress, and preferences stay in **your browser only**.

### Where do player photos come from?
Public beta uses placeholders by default. Photos ship only when we have **licensed assets**—no scraping or hotlinking.
